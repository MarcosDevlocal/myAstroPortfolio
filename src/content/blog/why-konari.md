Konari exists because technology should not be a luxury good. Too many communities only experience software as something that happens *to* them — opaque systems, brittle portals, and dashboards built for someone else's KPIs.

The nonprofit model gives us permission to optimize for dignity:

- **Clarity over cleverness** — if a volunteer can run it on a Tuesday night, it ships
- **Partnerships over press releases** — outcomes belong to the people we serve
- **Sustainability** — small, maintainable tools beat hero projects that rot in six months

Running Konari alongside my engineering career keeps me honest. It reminds me that reliability, documentation, and empathy are not "soft" skills — they are how software earns trust.
